# Relevance Specification - Example

This is a generic example of a relevance specification. Replace this with your actual research focus.

## Core Focus

- Example research area 1
- Example research area 2
- Example research area 3

## Priority Topics

### High Priority
- Topic 1
- Topic 2
- Topic 3

### Medium Priority
- Related topic 1
- Related topic 2

## Exclusions

- Unrelated area 1
- Unrelated area 2

## Modalities of Interest

- Data type 1
- Data type 2

## Methods of Interest

- Method 1
- Method 2

## Must-Have Signals

- Paper explicitly addresses [your topic]
- Paper proposes [specific approach]

## Likely Irrelevant Signals

- Paper focuses on [unrelated area]
- Paper is about [excluded topic]

## Relevance Scoring Rubric

**Score 5 — Must-read**
- Directly addresses core focus
- Uses methods of interest
- Satisfies multiple must-have signals

**Score 4 — Strong relevance**
- Addresses related topics
- Relevant methodology
- Satisfies at least one must-have signal

**Score 3 — Tangential**
- Mentions related concepts
- Could be adapted to your area

**Score 2 — Weakly related**
- Distant connection
- Different focus

**Score 1 — Irrelevant**
- Matches exclusion criteria
- No connection to research area
